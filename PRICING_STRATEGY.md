# RiskShield AI Pricing Strategy

## Current public plans

Keep the current plan ladder:

- Starter: $49/mo
- Growth: $249/mo
- Scale: $1499/mo

The prices stay the same, but the value framing changes from per-email verification to agency audit capacity and client delivery.

## New pricing unit

Price the product around:

- contacts audited
- list audit reports
- client-ready reports
- agency workflow
- API / white-label / audit history

The unit of value is not "cheap email verification."

## Recommended monthly allowances

Suggested baseline entitlements:

- Starter: 500 contacts audited / month, 2 audit reports
- Growth: 2,500 contacts audited / month, 10–15 audit reports
- Scale: 15,000 contacts audited / month, 60–100 audit reports

These are recommended planning numbers and can be tuned later based on actual usage and margin.

## What to avoid in public pricing

Do not foreground:

- $0.10 per email
- low-cost cleaning
- generic verification comparisons
- commodity benchmark wars against ZeroBounce or NeverBounce

Do not sell the product as a cheap list checker.

## How to explain value

Public messaging should emphasize:

- campaign launch safety
- contacts audited before launch
- risk reduction before spend
- client-ready reports for agencies
- workflow visibility for outbound teams

## Plan differentiation

Starter:

- small outbound teams
- light agency usage
- basic audit output

Growth:

- active agencies
- recurring client delivery
- stronger report volume and workflow depth

Scale:

- high-volume agencies
- multiple client accounts
- audit history, API, and white-label-adjacent workflow needs

## Pricing narrative

The buyer should feel:

- "This saves campaign waste and protects deliverability."
- "This is worth paying for before a launch."
- "This is a client-facing deliverable, not a utility."
